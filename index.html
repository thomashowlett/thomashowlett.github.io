Industrial Technology: Multimedia Major Work

(Aug. 2017) 'CTRL///'was my first real project and was undertaken in the Year 12 HSC Industrial Technology course requiring the submission of a major work, accompanying folio and seperate video evidencing the construction of the project. In the division of Industrial Technology: Multimedia, I chose to engage with my audience on the topic of internet security through the medium of a short film/music video, combining a wide range of visual effects and animation elements. Follow this link to view the 4 minute long video, accompanying 40 page A3 Folio, 'Behind the Scenes' video and Hand coded website submitted by myself to the NSW Education Standards Authority (NESA) for marking.
Whilst not my first work showcasing learnt video editing, cinematography and animation 'skills', I believe this was my first real attempt at the creative world and the multitude of awards and nominations that were as a result of the project really opened up my eyes to an unforeseen career path I now wanted to pursue.

Work Experience at Headjam

(July. 2017) Now showing much more interest in the subject and in between working on a nine-month long major project, i was fortunate enough to have been allowed to attend 7 days work experience at perhaps Newcastle's most infamous Creative Agency; Headjam. The few members of the team at Headjam each specialised in their own area of skills and i was able to pickup a few tips from the studio's Web Designer, Graphic Designer, Broadcast Designer and Account Manager each demonstrating what it is like to be employed by an Ad. Agency.

Wollongong STEM Awards

(Dec. 2016) After completion of the Preliminary (Year 11) Industrial Technology course, I submitted my individual assignment task consisting of and titled; 'Weather Report' to the Annual Wollongong State STEM Awards. Not expecting any repercussions, myself and one other student in the class were called to attend the awards evening to recieve awards for our projects. I ended up claiming the title of 'Multimedia Technologies Focus Area Award Winning Student' and $150 prize money which i put towards purchasing my first DSLR camera.
